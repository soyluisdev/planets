Our Star
Sun
The Sun, a class G star at the solar system's center, emits light and energy crucial for life on Earth, with solar eruptions and a steady stream of charged particles influencing the entire solar system.
1st Planet
Planet
Mercury
Mercury, the closest planet to the Sun, displays a rugged, cratered terrain, experiencing extreme temperatures ranging from scorching daytime heat to glacial cold at night. Its proximity to the Sun influences its rocky landscape, making it an intriguing subject for scientific study.
2nd Planet
Planet
Venus
Venus, Earth's "sister planet," boasts a dense atmosphere and blistering climate, with surface temperatures high enough to melt lead.
3rd Planet
Planet
Earth
Earth, our home planet, hosts life with its oxygen-rich atmosphere, vast oceans, and unparalleled biodiversity.
4th Planet
Planet
Mars
Mars, the "Red Planet," features a thin atmosphere and is marked by canyons, mountains, and dry riverbeds, fueling scientific interest in the search for life.
5th Planet
Planet
Jupiter
Jupiter, the gas giant, sports a turbulent atmosphere and polar auroras, with an intriguing moon system including Europa, potentially harboring oceans beneath its icy surface.
6th Planet
Planet
Saturn
Saturn, renowned for its stunning rings of ice and rock, is a gas giant with storms and fierce winds, along with a captivating array of moons, including Titan.
7th Planet
Planet
Uranus
Uranus, an icy planet with a bluish atmosphere, features a tilted axis causing extremely long seasons, along with a ring system and moons that pique curiosity.
8th Planet
Planet
Neptune
Neptune, the farthest planet from the Sun, is another gas giant with a dynamic atmosphere and intriguing moons like Triton, exhibiting geological activity and a retrograde orbit.
Dwarf Planet
Planet
Pluto
Pluto, now classified as a dwarf planet, resides in the Kuiper Belt, featuring an icy surface and a moon, Charon, influencing its orbit, generating scientific interest since the New Horizons mission.
